Eating out

Family & social meals
Fri / Sat date night dinners
You're going to enjoy these — that's fine and healthy. The goal is to be intentional, not to suffer through a salad.
1
Eat a lean, high-protein snack before you leave — hard-boiled eggs or a protein shake. You'll order with more control when you're not hungry.
2
Order protein-forward — steak, salmon, chicken, shrimp. Share a side or an appetizer rather than ordering two full courses.
3
Skip the bread basket. Have a glass of wine if you want — just count it (~125 kcal, 4g carbs per glass).
4
If you get dessert, split it. A few bites satisfies the craving and keeps you from feeling deprived.
Sunday family dinners
You can't control what your mom or mother-in-law cooks. You can control how much you eat and how you build your plate.
1
Protein first, always. Whatever protein is on the table — chicken, beef, pork, turkey — fill half your plate with it before anything else.
2
Vegetable second. Whatever veg is available — even if it's got butter on it — take a big portion. It fills your plate and your stomach.
3
Starchy sides in moderation. Mashed potatoes, pasta, rice — take a small portion. One scoop, not three.
4
Eat slowly and stop at 80%. Family dinners run long — there's time between servings. Let satiety catch up before going back for seconds.
5
Earlier in the day, go lighter. Keep breakfast and lunch lean on Sundays knowing dinner will be bigger and less controlled.
6
Don't stress what you can't control. One family dinner a week will not derail 26 weeks of progress. Consistency over perfection.
